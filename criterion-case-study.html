Criterion

Building an app that makes it easy to search, purchase and read digital books + a CMS that helps users create, manage and modify content on the app.

Role

Design Lead | Front end Developer

Platform

Mobile / Web App

Services Provided

Research, Strategy, Design, Development

Background

Criterion is a digital store that makes it easy to buy, read, and manage your digital books. Criterion delivers over 500 digital books from different genres.

Design Challenge

The challenge was to develop an application where users can browse through different categories of books and get to read them on the same platform. In addition, find ways to create a great user experience in discovering, searching, and reading books. Apart from designing the application, I was also tasked with the design and static development of the CMS content management system. This is used to manage the creation and modification of digital content/book on the Criterion ebook store.

My Role

I was responsible for researching, brainstorming, mapping, wireframing, prototyping, and also front-end static coding. This project helped to deepen my knowledge in user experience design and front-end static development.

Discover

This was an opportunity to work with the product manager and explore the problem space. The output of this conversation between the product manager and I was a list of product requirements

  1. Product requirements

Some of the expected key features of the Criterion ebook store were

  • Add to cart: that allows users to choose items to purchase and gather into their basket.
  • Ratings and reviews: testimonials written by users who have purchased books and used the digital product or service. it would be accompanied by a rating (from 0 to 5).
  • Customizable book reader: with different options like the font size, font choice, and text alignment.
  • Search feature:that filters from the myriad of books on the platform.
  • Explore page: helps to explore different genres of books in the store.
  1. Competitive analysis

I reviewed our competitor’s overall app store ratings such as looking at the total number of downloads, user reviews and determining which features make them unique. These are the analysis I pulled from some digital ebook stores considering these are the common apps used by our potential users.

  1. User Journey

The journey mapping was an opportunity to compile a series of user actions into a timeline. This is where the team got to focus on a certain target/goal within the journey map.

Define phase/Synthesizing research

In this stage of the design process, I decided to take out the learnings from the competitive analysis and I did the feature prioritization, classifying them into the high-impact feature (must have) and low-impact feature (unexpected).

Develop phase

This was an opportunity to ideate on different ideas and concepts based on the product requirements.

  1. Paper prototype

With low-fidelity paper prototypes, the general structure of the application could easily be tested in usability tests. Without much effort, adjustments could be made before going into the much more costly digital implementation.

  1. Design Explanation and Insights

After some paper prototyping adjustments, high-fidelity prototypes were created, which I supplemented with clickability using Figma. Again, user tests with the product manager revealed small vulnerabilities in the structure of the user interface which led to further improvements.

  1. High-fidelity prototype

We focused on incorporating the changes that we have agreed on and I started consolidating them into final designs.

Content management system (CMS)

We also decided to create a content management system (CMS) that was used to manage the creation and modification of digital content on the book app.

Conclusion

What we did well

We moved fast. As the only designer on the project, I touched everything from app design to coding. Designing for so many screens successfully required a lot of attention to detail while keeping a very high level of communication throughout the entire design and development sprint. Each morning the entire team would do a stand-up, where we all synced on our tasks for the day and updates on our goals for the week. one of the best things that we did was to test the product internally. Doing so helped to identify the pain points of the app and quickly iterate based on employee feedback.
