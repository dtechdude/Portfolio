Web

Services Provided

Research, Strategy, Design, Development

Background

CASIA is a sophisticated, yet easy-to-use simple workflow application enabling the user to effectively design and evaluate fit-for-purpose well architectures. It is simply a well data record management tool. The target audiences are engineers, managers, subsurface engineers, and people in the engineering industry.

Design Challenge

The organization's website needed to be designed to better connect with its audiences (mostly engineers) including building an intuitive information architecture and developing a coherent visual language. My task was to work on an effective website that would transfer the company’s values and product benefits to the customers and help them take advantage of the offered services flawlessly.

My Role

I worked solely on the marketing website design from research to conception and visualization. I was also responsible for the front-end static coding. I worked closely with a product manager and one core front-end developer to build the marketing site that was not only aesthetically pleasing but also feasible with our tight deadlines.

Foundational research

  1. Product Manager Interview

In order to build a better understanding of the product, I conducted a one-on-one interview with the product manager that had previously worked on the web application. This helped establish the foundations for the project by providing valuable insights that would otherwise be difficult to obtain. I couldn’t conduct a user interview because of the time constraint but through the lens of the product manager, I was able to see the needs of users which helped in the alignment of the business and users' goals.

Interview questions

  • What should this marketing website accomplish for the business?
  • Who are the users? How would you describe the customers? How might we provide an experience that is engaging and valuable to the users?
  • What action should this deliverable entice the user to take?

Product Requirements gathered

  • Help target audience understand the benefits and features of the product. how the platform can help multiply their output.
  • Increment in conversion and retention rate.
  • Deliver information in a clear concise fashion.
  1. Competitive analysis

An extensive competitor analysis was conducted with both organizations directly related to CASIA's business model as well as indirect competitors. I audited the information architecture, layout, tone, and navigation of these platforms.

Develop

With the research phase completed, I started brainstorming ideas. This was an opportunity to ideate on different ideas and concepts based on the product requirements.

  1. Userflow

User flow is the path taken by a prototypical user on a website or app to complete a task. The user flow takes them from their entry point through a set of steps towards a successful outcome and final action. I started sketching out the user flow to help the team imagine how a user would navigate the website. It will also assist us in determining what the final goal is when a user is on the site. One of the challenges was identifying a final goal for a user as the website is content-heavy. In the end, we determined that the goal for each user is to land on either the Contact Us page (inspire visitors to reach out and connect) or click on the Access demo account button.

  1. Prototyping & Usability Testing

Several rounds of usability testing were undertaken at three different stages of the design process including.

  • Paper prototype which moved through a number of iterations.
  • Low-fidelity prototype that allowed us to take on board significant feedback.
  • High-fidelity prototype incorporating the desired imagery and content.

Paper prototype

With the paper prototype, the general structure of the website could easily be tested in usability tests. Without much effort, adjustments could be made before going into the much more costly digital implementation.

Low-fidelity prototype

We focused on incorporating the changes that we have agreed on and started consolidating them into our Low-fidelity sketches on the computer.

Key changes after testing low-fidelity prototype

  • A statistic section was initially created on the why us page, a trusted partner section was later introduced after talking with the client. This promotes higher trust when users land on the page.
  • An all black theme was the design direction at first but to be able to properly show the content and product, we opted for a mix of black and white.

High-fidelity prototype

We focused on incorporating the changes that we have agreed on and I started consolidating them into final designs.

Conclusion

Due to the constant feedback with the product manager, after the workshops hardly were any revisions required, neither in information architecture nor in visual design. Creating more design exploration for the testimonial section of the homepage would be something that I would have loved to work on more if I had more time.
